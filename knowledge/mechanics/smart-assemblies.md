# Smart Assemblies

Smart Assemblies are the player-built on-chain structures of EVE Frontier. They are the foundation of player-driven civilization — storage, defense, travel infrastructure, and processing are all built and maintained by players.

## Assembly Types

### Smart Storage Unit (SSU)
The most common assembly. Stores items in a shared, on-chain inventory. Other players can deposit and withdraw if the owner's access rules allow it. SSUs can have custom Sui Move extensions for programmable logic and custom dApp URLs that open in the game's browser.

### Smart Turret
Automated defense platforms. Owners program targeting rules (attack hostiles, protect allies, etc.) via on-chain logic. Turrets fire automatically at targets within range. Critical for defending valuable locations.

### Smart Gate
Player-built jump gates connecting systems. Creating travel infrastructure is one of the most impactful things a player can do. Gates are expensive to build but provide massive utility to the community.

### Refinery
Processes raw ore into refined materials. Required for advanced crafting. Building a Refinery is a mid-game milestone.

## On-Chain Nature

Every Smart Assembly exists as on-chain objects on Sui. They persist even when the owner is offline. Their state is verifiable and their extensions are programmable. This is what makes EVE Frontier's world truly player-owned — the infrastructure itself lives on-chain.

## Custom Extensions

Owners can deploy Sui Move packages as extensions to their assemblies, adding custom logic: access control, event logging, automated services, and more. This is how builders extend the game.
