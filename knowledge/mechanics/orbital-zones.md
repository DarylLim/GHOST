# Orbital Zones and Systems

EVE Frontier's universe is divided into systems connected by warp routes and player-built gates. Each system has distinct characteristics that affect gameplay.

## System Structure

Each system contains:
- **Orbital zones**: Areas around celestial bodies where players operate
- **Asteroid fields**: Minable resource deposits
- **Open space**: The void between zones — mostly empty, sometimes dangerous
- **Smart Assemblies**: Player-built structures anchored in space

## System Threat Levels

Systems have inherent threat levels based on distance from starter zones:
- **Low threat (0-25)**: Starter and adjacent systems. Few Feral AI, basic resources
- **Medium threat (26-50)**: Transitional zones. More hostiles, better resources
- **High threat (51-75)**: Dangerous frontier. Strong Feral AI, rare resources
- **Extreme threat (76-100)**: Deep space. Maximum hostiles, richest resources, highest risk

## Navigation

- Use the System Map (Tab) to view connected systems and their threat levels
- Warping between zones within a system costs fuel
- Traveling between systems requires gates (player-built) or direct warp routes
- Always plan your route before departing — know your fuel budget and return path
