# Route Planning

Successful exploration and survival in EVE Frontier requires planning your routes carefully. A poorly planned trip can leave you stranded in hostile space.

## Fuel Budgeting

Before any multi-system trip:
1. Check current fuel level and maximum
2. Count the number of warps to your destination
3. Count the return warps (assume same route unless you know otherwise)
4. Add 2 emergency warps to the budget
5. If total required > current fuel, refuel first

Formula: Required fuel = (outbound warps + return warps + 2 emergency) * average warp cost

## Route Safety

- Check threat levels of all systems on your route via the system map
- Prefer routes through lower-threat systems even if longer
- Note which systems have player-built gates (faster travel)
- Identify SSUs along the route where you can refuel or dock in emergency

## When Plans Go Wrong

If you find yourself low on fuel in an unknown system:
- Immediately stop all non-essential activity
- Scan for nearby SSUs that might offer fuel
- Move to the least trafficked area if hostile activity is high
- If truly stranded, ask for help in community channels before self-destructing

## Deep Space Expeditions

For trips into high-threat space:
- Bring Fuel Cells in cargo (backup fuel supply)
- Travel with other players if possible
- Set waypoints on your return route
- Accept that you may lose your Shell — don't carry irreplaceable items
